Card Conditions Explained

Magic cards don't generally thrive off of being played. Shuffling, playing, and windmill-slamming cards tends to leave marks, and even the most cautious player can be caught unaware when a glass of soda is spilled over the battlefield accidentally. To prevent disappointment, we expect every user to indicate what the condition of their offered cards is.

Mint

Mint cards are booster-fresh. Placed into a protective sleeve directly after being taken out of the booster, this card has no visible abnormalities, has no scratches or deformities, and its border is a uniform color. It even smells nice.

Near-mint

A card that spent a few moments being sorted through in a pile, from which it sustained a minimal amount of damage in the form of slight scuffing or clouding. Still considered 100% value.
